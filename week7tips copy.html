Factors like elevation gain, rapidly changing weather, and the sun's intensity can make things a little bit different when hiking in Colorado compared to other places. Here are some tips to be prepared for on a Colorado hike:

  • Be aware of altitude sickness. It is much harder to breathe at elevations around 7,000 feet and up because the atmospheric pressure and oxygen percentage decrease at a rapid rate.
  • Be prepared for the weather to change quickly. Afternoon storms in the summer are common and can bring heavy rain, lightning and hail. Hiking early is best.
  • Colorado has a dry climate and it is easy to become dehydrated. A good rule of thumb is to drink one liter of water every two hours while hiking.
  • It's likely that you will encounter wildlife on your hike. Please remember these animals are wild and should not be approached. Stay calm, make your presence known, and keep your distance.
  • With thinner atmosphere and higher elevation, the sun is intense! Wear sunglasses and plenty of sunscreen.
